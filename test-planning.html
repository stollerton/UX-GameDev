Test Session Planning
Planning the details of the usability testing session is, in some ways, the most crucial part of the entire process. The decisions you make at the start of the testing process will dictate the way you proceed and the results you end up with. Below are six important things to keep in mind while planning.
1. Define the Area of Focus
Before you do anything else, you need to determine what is the purpose of the test? What areas of your game would benefit the most from usability testing? Once you have decided on that you can move forward.
2. Who are Your Participants?
Typically, the participants should represent the players of your game, but you may want to narrow down more specifically on a certain user segment in order to identify a specific problem.
3. The Questions
Make sure you have a list of the specific questions you want to ask users about your game after or during the testing session. What are you trying to find out about your game?
4. Location
Where you will do the testing is important, it should be reasonable for your participants to get to and feel comfortable in, with as few distractions as possible. Remote testing through software is also possible.
5. Timetable
Planning when the testing session is crucial, especially if you have deadlines to meet. Take into considerations limitations of your participants, availability, location constraints, and other time depenancies.
6. Moderators
You will need to decide who will run the testing sessions? Moderating user testing without influencing the results requires skill and practice. At its most basic, a hands off and unbiased approach is best.
7. Recording Setup
Take advantage of video and audio recording, in an ideal situation, you want to set up to record the participants’ screens, their speech, and also their body language. Don’t forget to take notes!
